I am a Ph.D. candidate in Jeff Magee's Lab at Baylor College of Medicine. I graduated from Rhodes College with a B.S. in Neuroscience, where I was in the lab of Kelly Dougherty. For more information, refer to my CV.

My primary research interest is to identify mechanistic principles underlying intelligent behavior in the brain. I structure my thinking at two conceptual levels:

H1: How does the brain learn? An important aspect of intelligence is the brain's ability to change itself with experience. How does the brain change with experience, and how is this change useful in guiding adaptive behaviors?

H2: What is the biophysical implementation of learning in the brain? Learning an adaptive behavior may involve modifications to many brain regions. However, the computation is implemented at the biophysical level of individual synapses, dendrites, and neurons. What biophysical mechanisms adaptively modify the input-output transformations of neurons?
